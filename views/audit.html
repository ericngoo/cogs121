UCSD Degree Audit Redesign

Modernizing the Age Old Audit

Jan 2021 - Mar 2021

Role - Design Lead

Overview


Background

Planning classes shouldn’t be a pain!

During the winter of 2021, my team and I set out to address one of the biggest burdens that no one talks about at UCSD, the Degree Audit. It's a document that students have to refer to every quarter while planning out classes. It's the official academic document that tells a student what courses are uncompleted, how many units a student has completed, and even includes a small list of possible courses to take. My team and I found it interesting that with such an important academic document that is used by every student, a large amount of mutual friends and students still hated how it was designed. With this goal in mind, we set out to rethink the way that degree audit was designed!

The Problem

Planning classes is already a chore to most students, so why make the experience even worse with a completely tedious document?

Being a student is hard. A lifeless, monotonous degree audit to accompany those golden years in college is even worse. The current UCSD Degree Audit makes organizing your 4 year plan and course scheduling dreadful. As the years go on, countless students will suffer eye strain and stress while scanning up and down their plain degree audit unless a more organized, and modern design comes along to make the experience much more enjoyable.


Solution

UCSD students need a clearly organized degree audit in order to plan classes more effectively.

EZ-Audit is a collaborative project meant to bring some life and creativity to the age old UCSD Degree Audit. The goal of EZ-Audit is to make interaction with the Degree Audit intuitive and motivating by re-organizing the flow of information as well as logically grouping related data into different pages for the user to view as modular sections.
EZ-Audit’s biggest goal is to make a student’s journey towards graduation more enjoyable. It addresses many common issues that students have long complained about. Some of the most important issues brought up my friends and colleagues were that the audit was very plain and boring, there was no easy way to get through the audit without scrolling up and down for a long time, and each section was very unclear since there were no distinct colors/breaks in the titles. EZ-audit addresses this by offering a fresh and modular design that separates important sections of data into distinct sections.

Some Key Insights:

  1. Too plain! Too much scrolling! Too little colors!
    The Degree Audit is naturally very long and contains a large amount of academic history related data, but that is no excuse to make the document just one long page! It's important to create modular groups of data that have enough color for a student to easily distinguish sections.

  2. Not enough compatibility with UCSD's Webreg for course planning
    Student's commonly use the degree audit while planning classes, so compatibility with Webreg is very important in bridging the two separate tools offered by UCSD.

  3. Degree Audit should motivate students, not deter them
    College is not easy! Looking at one's degree audit should be a motivating experience that motivates them to keep pushing forward and try their best!

Process


Audience

Development

Pictured here is the current UCSD Degree Audit. As you can see, it's very plain and goes against all of the key insights!

Starting this project, one of the first things we set out to address was motivation and compatiblity with the rest of UCSD's services. For ease of access, my team decided that this application could be accessed through a UCSD Student's TritonLink Login information since EZ-Audit was meant to replace the existing Degree Audit.

Secondly, we wanted to draft ideas of making the degree audit more motivational as early as possible, since that was the key feature that made EZ-Audit more unique and refined for UCSD Students! Our main inspiration was imagining undergrad as a path up a mountain. There are many hills you have to overcome and checkpoints you can reach along the way, and generally getting your degree would be the same as reaching the peak of the mountain!

As we started designing ideas for the main audit page and the journey page, which represented the motivational aspect of the app, we received very insightful feedback. First, was that our team was trying to address too many issues brought up by students! This realization was important because it helped our team narrow down on the key insights, which were to redesign the audit and offer a motivational page to the audit. Second, after many requests, it seemed smarter to develop a mobile layout since most students use their smart phones for everything. Lo-fi Prototyping

As we designed our low-fidelity prototypes, we kept the key insights in mind. The first one was to incorporate more color to the audit in order to create easily discernible sections of data. Second, was to limit the amount of scrolling a student would have to do by creating intuitive scrollable sections of data. Lastly, we wanted to create an experience that motivated students, instead of deterring them.

Lo-fi Wireframe 1

Lo-fi Wireframe 2

However, one of the most critical feedbacks we received was our ignorance of compatibility to WebReg. One of the key goals of EZ-Audit was to enable students by making course planning more accessible through the Degree Audit. So one of the most important updates we made to the final prototype was integration with WebReg.

Final Prototype

As we polished our design and finalized our prototypes, we received various feedback about our selection of colors and how it made it difficult to read since the colors were harsh and looked tacky. So as we finished up coding our final prototype, we migrated much of our designs to use Bootstrap since it is a very trustworthy library that can modernize websites. We shifted our colors to be more pastel and dull so that we could highlight important sections of data, while not taking away from the information itself.

A fun addition we added to the Journey Page was that users were now able visually see their progress in the top left corner. This was a highly requested feature from almost all of the groups that evaluated our prototypes, because it was a clear visuzliation of a student's journey in terms of units completed. Another crafty addition was a motivational quote to remind students that it's not about the mountain or journey you conquer, but yourself! We found these additions to be strong features that helped create a motivational aspect to the application.

Final Prototype 1

Final Prototype 1

For our Main Audit Page, we found that a simple tabluar design would work best for the needs of the user. Since students only need a more intuitive/modular layout, there was no need to go overboard with design and colors. We decided that these simple grey and blue-greys would suffice and still bring eye attention to the focal points of the website. The dropdowns help tidy up the abundance of information but still allow users to scroll when they wish to read more! The navigation bar at the bottom of the page allows users to view splits of data with respect to classes that are still required, classes that are completed, and classes that are currently in progress.

Reflection

Building this project exposed me to the dedication and patience required to carry out a full design process with a team of designers. I learned the importance of communicating with not only your team but with the stakeholders, or in this case TA's. This was an extremely useful lesson because I found myself having to re-explain my thoughts and ideas more than once so that they were clear and straightforward, unveiling that learning how to convey thoughts/ideas in teams is important.
I learned that constructive criticism is much more insightful than embarassing! As a new designer with flaws, I appreciated getting honest feedback from my peers and TA's because it shined light on areas of my work that can be improved. It was difficult learning how to improve on these areas, but I have come to terms that learning the design process is not an easy task, and that I can only improve by going through it over and over again until I am more comfortable.
Overall, I was very enlightened after completing this project on a strict deadline, as it gave me a simulation of how a product is designed, developed, and delivered.
